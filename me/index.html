Siema!

I’m an Electrical Engineer who traded the scenic landscapes of Poland for the… well, slightly more unpredictable weather of the UK. Since making the move, I’ve spent over 12 years navigating the world of Building Services Consulting.

If there’s a cable in a wall or a distribution board keeping a skyscraper running, I’ve likely had a professional disagreement over it with the architect at some point.

Why this blog?

Well, I mean - why not?

They say you can’t teach an old dog new tricks, but they never said anything about stubborn grumpy git. This site is my little hobby where I’m aiming to achieve three things:

Knowledge Exchange: I’ve gathered a decade’s worth of "lessons learned" (code for: "I made the mistakes so you don't have to"). I want to share that while picking up some things and plugging gaps in my knowledge along the way.

The Great Connection: Engineering can sometimes feel like shouting into a void which is also known as a DTM. I’m here to connect with fellow consultants, and to let them know that noone knows what anyone is doing and yet things somehow happen - so take a chill pill.

Dusting off the Code: My web development skills date back to many moons ago. If the site looks a bit retro, let’s call it vintage aesthetic while I re-learn how to tell a < div > from a hole in the ground.

"The transition from 'it works' to 'I know why it works' is where the magic happens"
